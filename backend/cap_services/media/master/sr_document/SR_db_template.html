The Benefits of an ISA

Putting money into an ISA is extremely tax efficient, as any growth will be free from Capital Gains Tax, and future income will be able to be taken free of income tax. If you normally complete a tax return, you won’t need to declare any ISA interest, income or capital gains on it. Additionally, the tax protected status of an ISA can be passed to a spouse on death. For these reasons, an ISA is very often used as a first port-of-call to take advantage of these valuable tax benefits. It is sensible to try and utilise as much of the ISA allowance as possible each tax year, as if you don’t use it in any one tax year, (which runs from 6th April to 5th April the year after) then you lose it.

Glossary of Terms

This is a charge levied by the Investment Manager for managing an investment fund. The charge is intended to compensate the managers for their time and expertise. Commodities

Equities

An ownership interest in a corporation in the form of common stock or preferred stock.

Gilts

Bonds that are issued by the British government and generally considered low risk.

Hedge Fund

A managed portfolio of investments that uses advanced investment strategies such as leveraged, long, short and derivative positions in both domestic and international markets with the goal of generating high returns

Investment Fund

Where pooled funds of retail investors are invested with the expectation of a profit for a fee. By aggregating the funds of a large number of small investors into a specific investments (in line with the objectives of the investors), an investment company gives individual investors access to a wider range of securities than the investors themselves would have been able to access.

Private Equity

Equity capital that is not quoted on a public exchange. Private equity consists of investors and funds that make investments directly into private companies or conduct buyouts of public companies that result in a delisting of public equity. Capital for private equity is raised from retail and institutional investors, and can be used to fund new technologies, expand working capital within an owned company, make acquisitions, or to strengthen a balance sheet.
